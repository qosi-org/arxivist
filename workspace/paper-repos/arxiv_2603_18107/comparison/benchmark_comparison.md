**No user experimental results were submitted for this comparison run**, and unlike a typical
paper, exact reproduction faces a **structural** obstacle beyond the usual missing-data
inconvenience: DSLOB — the dataset behind this paper's single headline claim (64.96%
directional accuracy) and its entire ablation study (Table 3) — is generated from a "real
high-frequency limit order book dataset" that Section 3.4 never names, cites, or identifies
in any way. No one, including the paper's own future collaborators without access to the
authors' private data, can exactly reproduce DSLOB from the paper alone.

Fabricating a score from the Stage 4/5 placeholder-seed smoke tests would be actively
misleading: any deviation would reflect (a) the placeholder's different statistical
properties from any real market data, and (b) toy-scale training (small dims, few epochs),
not implementation correctness. See `verification_log.md` for full rationale and
`reproducibility_score.json` for the disposition (`requires_manual_review: true`).

## Root Cause Analysis

Not applicable in the usual sense (no matched metric pairs to analyze deviations for). The
closest analogue: if a future comparison on Jane Street/Optiver/Time-IMM shows large
deviations, the highest-leverage things to check first, in order, are:

1. **H-1 (this report)**: confirm `model.artemis.d_w == model.artemis.d_z` in your config —
   with the shipped defaults, `L_MPR` silently evaluates to 0 throughout training.
2. **P-1/P-3/P-4** (see `hallucination_report.md`): latent dimension, SDE step count, and all
   four loss weights are unreported by the paper and are ArXivist-authored defaults.
3. Training duration/batch size (P-9): the paper only confirms 10 epochs for the DSLOB
   ablation specifically (Figure 6); main Table 2 benchmark training length is unstated.

## Recommended Actions

Prioritized by expected impact:

1. **Fix H-1 before any training run**: set `d_w = d_z` in `configs/config.yaml`, or the
   market-price-of-risk loss will silently be zero, making any run closer to the paper's own
   `A3_NoMPR` ablation variant than to its full model.
2. **Obtain Jane Street / Optiver / Time-IMM data** (all public, see `data/README_data.md`)
   and run the full 5-model × 5-seed benchmark on these three datasets — this is the only path
   to a real, non-`N/A` reproducibility score for this paper, since DSLOB cannot be obtained.
3. **Do not attempt to claim DSLOB reproduction** as evidence of fidelity either way; at best,
   a *qualitatively* similar synthetic dataset could be built from a different, actually-named
   real LOB dataset (e.g. LOBSTER) fed through the already-validated `DSLOBGenerator`
   pipeline, clearly labeled as an analogous-but-not-identical dataset.
4. **When real results are available**, prioritize the DSLOB-analogue ablation study first —
   it's the paper's most information-dense result (7 variants, clean directional signal) and
   the mechanism (PDE/MPR loss ablation) is fully implemented and Stage-4/5-validated.
